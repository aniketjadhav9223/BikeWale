Heritage-Classic key highlights

Harley-Davidson Heritage Classic is a cruiser bike available in only 1 variant and 4 colours. Heritage Classic price starts at Rs. 26,59,000 in India. The Harley-Davidson Heritage Classic is powered by 1868cc BS6 engine which develops a power of 93.87 bhp and a torque of 155 Nm. With both front and rear disc brakes, Harley-Davidson Heritage Classic comes up with anti-locking braking system. This Heritage Classic bike weighs 330 kg and has a fuel tank capacity of 18.9 liters. The 2023 Harley-Davidson Heritage Classic is updated with new colour schemes. There is Vivid Black, Prospect Gold, Bright Billiard Blue, and Heirloom Red Fade. From the entire palette offered for the Heritage Classic, the Heirloom Red Fade is not only the most expensive but also seems to be the most desirable option. Harley-Davidson has powered the 2023 Heritage Classic with a 1,868cc V-twin engine that makes 94bhp and 155Nm. It’s mated to a six-speed gearbox. Harley claims that this bike can return 5.5 litres/100km (18.18kmpl) of fuel economy. The bike rides on 49mm telescopic front forks and monoshock with hydraulic preload adjustability. Meanwhile, the braking hardware comprises a single front and rear disc mounted on cast aluminium front and rear wheels. Harley-Davidson has equipped the Heritage Classic with LED illumination, and an analogue console with a digital inset. The instrument cluster packs various readouts like the odometer, trip meter, speedometer, and gear position indicator.

Harley-Davidson Sportster S key Summary

Harley-Davidson Sportster S is a cruiser bike available in only 1 variant and 4 colours. Sportster S price starts at Rs. 18,79,000 in India. The Harley-Davidson Sportster S is powered by 1252cc BS6 engine which develops a power of 120.69 bhp and a torque of 125 Nm. With both front and rear disc brakes, Harley-Davidson Sportster S comes up with anti-locking braking system. This Sportster S bike weighs 225 kg and has a fuel tank capacity of 11.8 liters. The Harley-Davidson Sportster S sports the same brawny styling as before. It features a distinctive design that comprises a single-pod headlight, a compact fuel tank, a single-pod instrument cluster, a flat handlebar, a rider-only saddle, a twin-pod exhaust, and fat-profile tyres. The minimal body panels ensure that the Revolution Max 1250T engine is fully visible. This 1,252cc, V-twin, liquid-cooled engine is tuned to deliver a maximum output of 120.6bhp at 7,500rpm and a peak torque of 125Nm at 6,000rpm. Now, the motorcycle is offered in a single variant and four colours – Vivid Black, White Sand Pearl, Grey Haze, and Bright Billiard Blue. The comprehensive feature list of the 2023 Sportster S comprises full-LED lighting, a Bluetooth-enabled colour TFT display that works with a dedicated smartphone application, a USB charger, and a plethora of rider aids. These include ABS with a cornering function, lean-sensitive traction control system, cornering drag-torque slip control system, and wheel lift mitigation function. Meanwhile, the Bluetooth module brings access to phone calls, music, and navigation. As for hardware, the suspension tasks on the motorcycle are handled by 43mm upside-down front forks and a rear monoshock. The front forks and rear monoshock are adjustable for compression, rebound, and preload. The braking tasks are handled by a single disc with a four-piston calliper at the front and a single rotor with a single-piston calliper at the back.

Harley-Davidson PAN America 1250 Summary

Harley-Davidson PAN America 1250 is a adventure bike available in only 1 variant and 6 colours. PAN America 1250 price starts at Rs. 24,49,000 in India. The Harley-Davidson PAN America 1250 is powered by 1252cc BS6 engine which develops a power of 150.19 bhp and a torque of 128 Nm. With both front and rear disc brakes, Harley-Davidson PAN America 1250 comes up with anti-locking braking system. This PAN America 1250 bike weighs 258 kg and has a fuel tank capacity of 21.2 liters. Harley-Davidson has taken the standard variant of the Pan America off its portfolio and is just offering the Special trim in India. This variant of the ADV can be opted for either with alloy wheels or spoke wheels. Notably, the latter comes with tubeless tyres and demands a premium pricing over the former. Strangely, the American bike maker has kept the 2023 Pan America 1250 Special unaltered on all other fronts. Powering the motorcycle is a 1,252cc, twin-cylinder, liquid-cooled engine that produces 150.9bhp at 8,750rpm and 128Nm of peak torque at 6,750rpm. The electronics package is as impressive as the engine with features like lean-sensitive traction control, cornering ABS, electronically linked braking system, hill hold control, cruise control, engine braking control, five pre-programmed riding modes, and three customisable modes. Moreover, Harley has also bestowed the bike with electronically adjustable semi-active suspension which allows tweaking the USDs and monoshock as per the riding conditions. In India, the 2023 Harley-Davidson Pan America 1250 competes against the likes of the BMW R 1250 GS, Honda Africa Twin, and Ducati Multistrada V4.
